About BFA

BFA

Builders of Future Africa (B.F.A.) is a non-governmental organization registered by the government of Tanzania act of 2002 under registration number00NGO/0009237 to operate in Tanzania main land and accredited by the International Youth Federation (IYF) with accreditation number AO/001213 for regional and International accreditation.

The aim of the organization is to build a prosperous Africa by restoring hope to children who have lost it and helping them meet their own essential needs. The organization was established by passionate youth in 2012 in Arusha (who were A-level students at Cornerstone Leadership Academy by then) after observing high levels of poverty and the low life standards of children in the local communities which has consequently led to problems such as lack of scholastic materials,poor parental care,lack of food, shelter and clothing as well as horrifying life testimonies. All these problems combined called for a collective effort to come up with solutions thus the birth of Builders of Future Africa (B.F.A).
B.F.A is currently sponsoring over 150 children and out of them 30 are being fully sponsored and the rest are partially sponsored. The sponsorship covers for the school uniforms, scholastic materials, school fees as well as health insurance on annual basis. Other forms of support include academic assistance and character development programs alongside fun games, sports and consultations which take place every Saturday evening at the Academyâ€™s campus. Recently, the organization has extended its works to other cities such as Dar es Salaam, Morogoro and Dodoma with the growing number of members as well as well-wishers across the country.

Objectives

  1. Number one

    Fighting poverty by educating the young generation and encouraging them to work hard and to follow their dreams.

  2. Number two

    To help the needy African children know their rights and that their basic needs are met.

  3. Number three

    To assist the needy children in African communities have access to quality health services and sanitation.

  4. Number four

    We aim at instilling good and socially acceptable character qualities in children for the general transformation of African communities.

  5. Number five

    To spread the message of Love, Hope and Faith to the community through children them being the foundation for change.

  6. Number six

    Avoiding conflicts teaching the value of peace, reconciliation above all sorts of stereotypes and by encouraging them to embrace unity in diversity.

Vision

To build a prosperous Africa where the people are "livers" rather than "survivors".

Mission

We are aiming at building hope among Africans who have lost hope through extending the good news.
